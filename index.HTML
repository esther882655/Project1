Welcome To Bagan

Bagan is located on the banks of the Irrawaddy River in Central Myanmar (formerly known as Burma) and is the site of the densest collection of Buddhist temples, stupas and monasteries in the world.

History Of Bagan

Bagan, an ancient city in Myanmar's Mandalay region, was the capital of the Pagan Kingdom from the 9th to 13th centuries. This kingdom unified the regions that would later form Myanmar. During its peak (11th-13th centuries), over 10,000 Buddhist temples, pagodas, and monasteries were built in the Bagan plains, with the remains of over 2,200 still standing today.

Famous Attraction In Bagan

Bagan is famous for its numerous and well-preserved Buddhist temples, pagodas, and stupas, with over 2,000 still standing in the Bagan Archaeological Zone. The area was once the capital of the Pagan Kingdom, and the temples are a testament to the rich history and artistic achievements of the period.

Things To Do In Bagan

Visiting temples like Ananda Temple, Shwezigon Paya, and Shwesandaw Pagoda, enjoying the views from the surrounding landscape, and exploring the local markets and streets.

Bagan's Traditional Food And Clothing

Bagan's traditional cuisine is known for its communal meals and unique blend of flavors, while traditional clothing includes the traditional longyi and other garments reflecting cultural and religious practices.
